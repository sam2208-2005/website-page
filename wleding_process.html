Welding Process

Explore different types of welding techniques used in engineering workshops for joining materials with precision and strength.

Arc Welding

Arc welding uses an electric arc to melt and join metals at a weld point. The intense heat generated by the arc melts the workpiece edges, which solidify upon cooling, forming a strong joint. Arc welding is versatile and can be used on various metals, making it popular in construction, shipbuilding, and industrial fabrication.

Electrode Welding

Electrode welding, also known as shielded metal arc welding (SMAW), involves using a consumable electrode coated in flux to lay the weld. The flux coating protects the molten weld pool from oxidation and contamination, ensuring a clean, strong bond. This method is commonly used for welding ferrous metals in steel structure and maintenance applications.

Spot Welding

Spot welding is a type of resistance welding where two metal surfaces are joined by applying pressure and heat from an electric current at small contact points. TIG Welding

TIG (Tungsten Inert Gas) welding, also known as Gas Tungsten Arc Welding (GTAW), uses a non-consumable tungsten electrode and an inert gas shield, typically argon, to protect the weld area from contamination. TIG welding is highly precise and used in applications that require clean, high-quality welds, such as aerospace and automotive manufacturing.
